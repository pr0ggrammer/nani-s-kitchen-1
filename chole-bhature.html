Chole Bhature

A beloved North Indian dish featuring spicy chickpea curry (chole) served with deep-fried, fluffy bread (bhature). A festive favorite!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Dried Chickpeas (or 1 can boiled)
  • 1 large Onion (chopped)
  • 2 Tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 tsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1 tsp Chole Masala
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp Oil
  • Fresh Coriander Leaves (for garnish)

  • For Bhature:
  • 1 cup All-Purpose Flour (Maida)
  • 2 tbsp Yogurt
  • 1/2 tsp Baking Powder
  • Pinch of Baking Soda
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (to knead)
  • Oil (for deep frying)

Instructions

  1. Soak chickpeas overnight. Pressure cook with salt until soft (or use canned).
  2. Heat oil, add cumin seeds. Sauté onion until golden.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste, then tomato puree. Cook till oil separates.
  4. Mix in turmeric, chili powder, and chole masala. Stir well.
  5. Add boiled chickpeas and simmer for 15–20 minutes. Adjust salt. Garnish with coriander.
  6. For bhature, mix flour, yogurt, baking powder, soda, and salt. Knead to soft dough. Rest 2 hours.
  7. Divide dough, roll into rounds, and deep-fry in hot oil until puffed and golden.
  8. Serve hot chole with freshly made bhature!
