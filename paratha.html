Paratha

A traditional Indian flatbread that’s crisp on the outside and soft inside. Often stuffed or served plain with curd, pickle, or chutney—perfect for breakfast or lunch!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Whole Wheat Flour
  • Water (as needed to knead)
  • 1/4 tsp Salt
  • 1 tsp Oil or Ghee (for the dough)
  • Ghee or Oil (for cooking)
  • Optional: Stuffing (boiled potatoes, paneer, etc.)

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix flour, salt, oil, and enough water to form a soft dough. Rest for 15–20 minutes.
  2. Divide the dough into equal portions. Roll each into a ball.
  3. (Optional) If stuffing, flatten one ball, add filling, seal and gently roll out again.
  4. Heat a tawa or skillet. Cook paratha on both sides until golden brown using a little ghee/oil.
  5. Serve hot with curd, butter, pickle, or chutney.
