Vada Pav

Mumbai’s iconic street food — a spicy potato fritter (vada) tucked inside a pav (bun), served with chutneys and fried green chili. Simple, spicy, and satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 4 Pav Buns
  • 2 large Potatoes (boiled and mashed)
  • 1 Green Chili (chopped)
  • 1 tsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1/2 tsp Mustard Seeds
  • 1/4 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp Oil
  • 8-10 Curry Leaves
  • For batter: 1 cup Besan (Gram Flour)
  • 1/2 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1/4 tsp Baking Soda (optional)
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for frying
  • Dry Garlic Chutney (optional)
  • Fried Green Chilies (for serving)

Instructions

  1. Heat oil, splutter mustard seeds, add curry leaves, ginger-garlic paste, green chili, turmeric, and mashed potatoes. Mix well.
  2. Let the mixture cool, then shape into lemon-sized balls.
  3. Prepare a smooth besan batter using water, salt, chili powder, and baking soda.
  4. Dip each potato ball into the batter and deep fry until golden.
  5. Slice pav buns, apply chutney, place the vada inside, and press gently.
  6. Serve hot with fried green chili and extra chutney.
